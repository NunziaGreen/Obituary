Their Story

A Life Remembered

Nunzia Green, renowned amateur chef and force of nature, passed away on June 22, 2026, in North Vancouver, following a long battle with lung cancer. She leaves behind her husband, Bill Green; children Cristina Green and Daniel Green and their spouses Wade Cherrington and Beverly Tang; grandchildren Joie, Petra, and Soleste; and a freezer full of frozen delicacies. She will be remembered for her commitment to community, her generosity, her sense of humor, and her joie de vivre.

Nunzia was born Annuziata Angelucci in Genazzano, Italy, an only child to Ben and Italia Angelucci. She moved to Canada at age nine but kept a strong connection to her Italian culture and to family who remained near Rome. Her first home in Canada was Ocean Falls, B.C., where she became a strong, accomplished swimmer as part of the town's famed swim-team culture. The family later moved to Trail, B.C., in 1964, where she finished high school.

In the fall of 1968, she moved to Vancouver to study Commerce at the University of British Columbia. It was there, in the Fort Camp residence, that she met Bill Green. They married on September 2, 1972 — rightfully overshadowing Game 1 of the Canada–Russia Summit Series — and honeymooned in Europe, mostly Italy, after Nunzia's graduation in 1973.

Together they built a life that moved from Calgary to Houston and back to Calgary before settling in Vancouver in 1979. Deep Cove became her beloved home for the rest of her life. After years at home with Cristina and Daniel, she returned to work in 1988 at Eastview Elementary School, later serving as secretary at Larson and Sherwood Park, and business manager at Carson Graham and Sutherland. She believed deeply in strong public schools and was known for going the extra mile for students, families, and staff.

She brought that same energy to her community — volunteering with the Deep Cove Parent Participation Preschool, the Mount Seymour Soccer Association, the North Shore Multicultural Society, and later the Harvest Project, alongside a lifelong support of the Vancouver Symphony. While Italian food was her religion, she took great pride in mastering cuisines from every culture, and her Christmas baking was legendary. She made more than twenty trips back to Italy, especially after 2001, staying close to family in Genazzano and Cave, and to her cousin Nives Angelucci in Rome. In 2025, she and Bill spent two months living in Rome, finally getting to experience daily life there as she had always wanted.

Nunzia's hospitality and generosity left their mark on thousands of people. She always rooted for the underdog and fought for those in need. Her force of will to make the world a better place will live on through the family, friends, and acquaintances whose lives she touched.
